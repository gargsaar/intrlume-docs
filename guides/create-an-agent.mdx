---

An **agent** is the voice AI that talks to your customers. Create one from the dashboard, test it, then point it at a campaign, a phone number, or your website.

## Before you start

Decide these first — Intrlume uses them to generate a better first draft:

| Decision | What to prepare |
| --- | --- |
| Channel | Outbound, inbound, or web |
| Industry & use case | e.g. lead qualification, appointment booking, reminders, support |
| Voice & language | A voice, a primary language, and an optional second language |
| Goal | One sentence on what the agent should achieve |
| Knowledge base *(optional)* | Documents the agent can answer from |

<Callout kind="info">
  New accounts don't have a phone number yet, so start with a **Web** agent — it works in the browser with no number. Add numbers later for phone calls.
</Callout>

## Step 1 — Open the agent builder

1. Sign in at [app.intrlume.tech](https://app.intrlume.tech).
2. Go to **Agents** in the sidebar.
3. Click **Quick Create** to create an agent.

## Step 2 — Choose a channel

Pick how people reach the agent.

| Channel | Use it when | Needs |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Outbound** | The agent calls your contacts | A provisioned phone number |
| **Inbound** | People call your number | A phone number (DID) linked to the agent |
| **Web** | Visitors talk to it on your website | Nothing — works instantly |

<Callout kind="tip">
  Choose **Web** to get started right away. Outbound and inbound agents need a phone number — email **admin@intrlume.tech** to have one added to your workspace.
</Callout>

## Step 3 — Pick an industry and use case

Choose your **industry**, then the **use case** that fits — lead qualification, appointment booking, reminders, support, and more. This tailors the generated prompt and conversation flow to your scenario.

## Step 4 — Fill in the details

| Field | What to enter |
| --- | --- |
| **Agent Name** | The name the agent introduces itself with |
| **Company Name** | Your business name |
| **Voice & Language** | A voice, a primary language, and an optional second language it can switch to |
| **Goal** | One or two sentences on what the call should achieve |
| **Knowledge Base** *(optional)* | Attach documents the agent can answer from |
| **Tone & Style** *(optional)* | How the agent should sound — e.g. "warm and conversational" |

Example goal:

```text
Book solar demo appointments, confirm time slots, and handle rescheduling in Hindi and English.
```

## Step 5 — Generate

Click **Generate Agent**. Intrlume writes the prompt, greeting, and conversation flow and builds the agent — the status moves from **generating** to **ready**, usually in moments. You'll land in the visual workflow editor, where you can refine any part.

## Step 6 — Test it

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Web call" icon="globe">
    For a Web agent, start a **web call** in the browser and talk to it live — no phone needed.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Test call" icon="phone">
    For a phone agent, use **Test Call** to have it ring your number.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

After a test, open **Call Logs** to see the transcript, recording, and captured data.

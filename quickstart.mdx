This guide takes you from zero to a working voice agent you can talk to.

## Before you start

<Callout kind="info">
  Sign up at [app.intrlume.tech](https://app.intrlume.tech). On your first sign-in you create an **organization** (your workspace) and become its **admin** — invite teammates anytime from **Settings → Members**. New organizations start on a **free trial** with credits, so you can build and test right away.
</Callout>

Trial accounts test with **web calls**, right in the browser. Phone calling — outbound and inbound — becomes available once you move beyond the trial and add a number.

## 1. Create an agent

In the dashboard, go to **Agents → New** and follow the builder.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pick a channel" icon="route">
    Choose **Outbound**, **Inbound**, or **Web**. New accounts don't have a phone number yet, so start with **Web** — it works instantly in the browser. You can add numbers later for phone calls.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose industry and use case" icon="layout-grid">
    Pick your **industry**, then the **use case** that fits — this tailors the agent to your scenario.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the details" icon="edit-3">
    Set the calling name, company, voice and language, and the agent's **goal** in plain language. Optionally attach a knowledge base or tools. For example:

    ```text
    Book solar demo appointments, confirm time slots, and handle rescheduling in Hindi and English.
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create" icon="zap">
    Click **Create**. Intrlume writes the prompt, greeting, and conversation flow and builds the agent in moments — the status moves from *generating* to *ready*. You can refine anything later in the visual workflow editor.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## 2. Test it in your browser

Open your **Web** agent and start a **web call** — no phone needed. Talk to it live; it handles interruptions and follows the flow you described.

After the call, open **Call Logs** to see the transcript, the recording, and any data the agent captured.

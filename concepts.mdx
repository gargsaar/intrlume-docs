Intrlume is built from a small set of concepts that fit together into one loop: you build an **agent**, give it a **workflow**, point it at an **audience**, run a **campaign**, and review the **calls** and **insights** that come back.

## The building blocks

- **[Agents](/concepts/agents)** — the AI that holds the conversation. Carries a voice, languages, a workflow, optional knowledge bases, and tools.
- **Workflows** — the conversation flow behind an agent: the steps and branches that decide what it says and does next.
- **Knowledge bases** — documents (PDF, URL, or text) the agent can search mid-call to answer questions accurately.
- **Tools** — actions the agent can take during a call, like looking up a record or booking a slot, through built-in or custom integrations.
- **Audiences & leads** — the contacts you call, uploaded as a CSV; each lead can carry custom fields used on the call.
- **Campaigns & batches** — a run of calls to an audience on a schedule, with retry rules and concurrency limits.
- **Calls & dispositions** — each conversation (outbound, inbound, or web), its outcome, and the data extracted from it.
- **Evaluation** — automatic scoring of call quality: faithfulness, hallucination, and topic adherence.

## How they connect

An **agent** runs a **workflow**. You point it at an **audience** and launch a **campaign**, which places **calls**. Every call is recorded, transcribed, and tagged with a **disposition**, and can be scored by **evaluation** — and those insights help you refine the agent and run again.
